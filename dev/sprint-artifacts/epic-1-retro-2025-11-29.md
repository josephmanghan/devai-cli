## Challenges & Growth Areas

### üîÑ Process Discipline Issues

1. **Linting Timing:** Linting was implemented at the end (Story 1-6) instead of at the beginning, requiring rework of previous stories
2. **Research Gaps:** Team proceeded with linting implementation without researching best practices first, causing delays
3. **Testing Scope Confusion:** Tests were written for infrastructure setup ("absolute garbage" and "arbitrary things") rather than focusing on business logic

### üîÑ Over-Engineering in Testing

The team spent excessive time testing infrastructure components that didn't need validation, extending the epic duration unnecessarily.

### üîÑ Insufficient Preparation

Failed to research linting approaches before implementation, leading to "pause and wait for deep research" moments.

## Key Insights & Learnings

### üí° Validation Beats Assumption

The `validate:setup` script providing concrete proof of foundation success was more valuable than any documentation or review process.

### üí° Process Sequence Matters

Linting and quality standards should be established FIRST, not last. Research should precede implementation.

### üí° Test Business Logic, Not Infrastructure

Focus testing efforts on actual business logic and user value, not infrastructure setup that provides little return on investment.

### üí° Styleguides Are Primary Research Sources

The team should reference existing `dev/styleguides/ollama-integration-patterns.md` rather than conducting ad-hoc research. The documentation already contains comprehensive patterns.

### üí° Automatic Processes Over Manual Tracking

Discipline should come from documented standards and automated systems, not manual tracking or individual memory.
